About

I am an Advanced Scientific Application Developer at the Department of Medical Physics, Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center (MSKCC), with 10+ years of experience in Bioinformatics, Image Analysis, and Machine Learning.

Projects

  • Software Tools

  • CERR (Key contributor)
    • The Computational Environment for Radiological Research (CERR) is a free, open-source research platform, used worldwide to prototype radiotherapy and image analysis algorithms (peer-review cited 700+ times). I've served as a core member of CERR's development team for 7+ years, leading the development of several key tools including for radiomics and radiotherapy outcomes analyses, building and deploying auto-segmentation models, and Python-compatibility via STRATIS.
  • ROE (Lead developer)
    • The Radiotherapy Outcomes Estimator, a plugin to CERR for patient-specific prescription selection, using published NTCP and TCP models to quantify the risks and benefits of different treatment protocols.

  • Radiomics (Lead developer)

    • I represented CERR at the Image Biomarker Standardization Initiative (IBSI), which aims to develop consensus defintions and guidelines for radiomic computations. This involved adapting our (free, open-source) tools to meet the proposed standard, establishing unit tests and cross-software comparisons for ongoing compliance.

  • Image segmentation (Lead engineer)

    • The deep-learning auto-segmentation tools I developed have been adopted for clinical use at MSK. As a result, we have revised guidelines for limiting induced complications through RT for head and neck cancer treatment, and improved clinical efficiency, accuracy, and reproducibility.
